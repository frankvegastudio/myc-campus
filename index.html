The program

Built for the gap between school and the real world

Independent studios and micro-manufacturers lose money every day to costing errors, slow quoting, and wasted material — not because designers aren't talented, but because they were never given the right tools.

Campus-to-Studio bridges that gap. Through a collaboration with design schools, Makers Yield & Costs (MYC) puts professional costing, yield modeling, and BOM documentation into the hands of students who are already solving these problems with spreadsheets and guesswork.

This isn't a classroom software license. It's a real-world collaboration between MYC, faculty, and a small cohort of students who want to be the first in their field to do this right.

The process

How Campus-to-Studio works

Designed around how design schools actually operate — not how software companies wish they did.

01

Faculty get early access

Participating faculty receive direct TestFlight (iOS) and Play beta (Android) access to MYC before any student involvement. No commitment, no agenda — just time to explore the tool on your own terms.

02

On-campus introduction

The MYC founder visits campus for a focused session — not a product demo, but a working conversation about costing and yield in real studio practice. Short. No slides. Honest.

03

Four to five students join

A small cohort of motivated students — selected together with faculty — receive full app access and become the program's founding cohort at their school.

04

Evangelists, not users

These students aren't beta testers. They're the people who will carry professional tools into their studios, into their teams, and into the industry when they graduate.

The research

This isn't marketing. It's a study.

Campus-to-Studio is grounded in graduate-level research measuring whether focused, designer-centric tools like MYC can meaningfully reduce costing errors, shorten quoting time, and improve material yield in small studio workflows.

Baseline data from faculty at FIT and partner schools documents the current state: studios are losing precision and time to manual processes. The program's student cohort will provide the first real-world app-assisted comparison data.

Participants contribute to both a product and a body of evidence that can shape how design schools approach operational tooling for the next generation of studio owners.
